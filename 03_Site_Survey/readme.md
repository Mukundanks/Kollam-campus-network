# Site Survey

## Overview
 A site survey was conducted with the help of aerial imaging and physical site knowledge (alumni), to understand the project in a bigger picture.

 The core agenda of the survey was to identify:
   * Geographical location
   * Boundaries
   * Existing network infrastructure
   * Road network
   * Possible obstacles
   * Distance from core
   * Future expansion possibilities
 The preliminary inspection showed that many institutions have shared boundaries, and 4 institutions are located close to the railway property.

## Survey Methodology
  Due to the highly conceptual nature of this project, a physical site visit is not possible. Instead, it was conducted based on data from Maps, aerial imaging, and most importantly my knowledge of the location when I was a student of one of the institutions. 

## Geographical Overview
  There are many reasons why I have chosen Kollam as my project location. This area is well-known to me, and being an alumni of one of the institutions, I have a good knowledge of the location. This location has around 9 institutions within the radius of less than 1KM, which makes an interconnect highly feasible.

  ## Environmental Risks
  * FMNC and Rail-DC are very close to each other, but the presence of more than 90% tree cover pose a significant threat to the overhead fiber cables. It was found that the trees are quite old, and there's possibility of broken branches falling off.
  * Church Zone is on the other side of DC-core. The road has also significant tree cover
  * The location is prone to high humidity, heavy rainfall and high salt-content in air from sea nearby
    
  ## Existing Infrastructure
  All the institutions listed here have their own network infrastructure to meet their daily needs. These will only serve less than 10% of their requirement, so everything is limited to where it is important or necessary. 
  1. Inspection at FMNC showed us there is existing wiring ducts and racks. The ducts cover more than 70% of the project in this site, but racks needs substantial upgrade in order to fulfill the requirements of this project
  2. BJEC is similar to FMNC, but they have dedicated physical rooms for network gears and possible future expansion. The building is relatively new, so it is designed with future demands in mind.
  3. BJ-Arc has limited network infrastructure. The inspection revealed this site requires a complete overhaul of their existing infrastructure.
  4. KRS's network infrastructure needs to be built from scratch
  5. SNT's infrastructure needs to be built from scratch
  6. SNL has limited infrastructure, so substantial work is required
  7. SNW has similar infrastructure  with SNL, needs bottom up work
  8. SNC has existing limited infrastructure , but room for future expansion. This brings a massive positive impact for the SN-zone side of project.
  9. DC has high speed backbone, but everything else needs to be built from scratch to achieve high-performance, and highly secure core.

 ## Fiber Route Assessment
 Once the existing and possible issues were figured out, the survey team did a preliminary analysis of potential fiber route. The factors considered were to reduce the cost of fiber, while minimising the environmental and physical impacts. It was decided that instead of individually deploying fibers to the building via FTTx, institutions who share the boundary will allow to lay fibers to their neighbour. This was a big win in this project as this can substantially reduce the capital expense. 
 1. To avoid possible road digging, high-quality outdoor fiber-cables will be deployed across DC and FMNC. FMNC will act as the main receiving end for all Church zone institutions and KRS. The planned route is underground cables from DC to the area close to FMNC boundary, change it to overhead over the roads and once in FMNC compound, underground cables. The cables will split to 4 and each of them will proceed to the respective institutions via undergound ducts. 
 2. The second zone and SNT will be covered by another fiber from DC, by overhead cables through the road. It will feed the SNT first, and the main line will split into 3 at SNL compound. From there, it will proceed to respective institutions via underground ducts.
 3. FMNC, BJEC and BJ-Arc needs special high-speed fiber interconnect to leverage the HPC cluster installing in future. Since the already laid fiber has enough bandwidth to cover this, it will be used to avoid extra cabling and costs.

 ## Site Constraints
 1.  70% of the buildings in this project is more than 30 years old. This makes laying new cables an extremely cautious job. To avoid potential building modifications like concrete drilling and cutting, existing racks or lightweight ducts or rackways will be used throughout. Presence of massive trees all over the campuses limits overhead cabling. These will be laid via underground ducts where required. 
 2. The sites are prone to heavy rain and flooding. This makes all outdoor units to be weatherproof rated and install it 3 feet higher if required.
 3. Limited current infrastructure to run the DC. Railway need to built a complete unit from scratch, which requires approval from various engineering teams within the company
 4. Cables crossing the road requires special permission. Cable taken though existing electricity poles needs special permission from the Electricity company
 5. Boundaries of many institutions are made with massive walls of stones from 1940s. Deploying underground ducts needs high-end equipments.

 ## Site Opportunities
  The main positive aspect of this project is the geographical locations of institutions and their interconnectivity. The railway DC is located at the heart of the city, and rail lines cover many other areas. This increases future expansions, and more clients becoming a part of this backbone. The DC can be expanded for future usage, giving railways the potential benefit of utilising their high capacity fiber infrastructure to expand beyond this location. 

 ## Survey Summary

 The survey concluded that this project is feasible and have future expansion potential, due to the geographical locations of the sites. All the participating institutions are close to each other, which makes interconnection easier. Various methods has been proposed in the survey to reduce capital and operational costs,  including underground and overhead cable conduits.
